## Justifications

**J1. Because verse 6 is elliptical, translate sahitam as predicated of the previously introduced manas/vijñāna, not of ālayavijñāna itself as the grammatical subject.**
- chosen: The supplied subject in translation is ‘this mind (manas)’, carried over from the previous verse. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [417, 418, 419]: `āha ca
avidyayā cātmadṛṣṭyā cāsmimānena tṛṣṇayā
ebhiś caturbhiḥ saṃkliṣṭaṃ mananālakṣaṇaṃ manaḥ` — And he says: ‘By ignorance, by self-view, by I-conceit, and by craving—by these four—the mind whose characteristic is mentation is defiled.’

**J2. The word excludes other caittas; it is not a general statement about all mental factors.**
- chosen: kleśaiḥ means ‘with afflictions’, a subset of mental factors. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [386, 387, 388]: `caittā hi dviprakārāḥ kleśās tad anye ca
tadanyebhyo vyavacchedārtham āha
kleśair iti` — Mental factors are of two kinds: afflictions and the others. To exclude those others, he says ‘with afflictions.’

**J3. Do not read this as a vague ‘fourfold’ epithet; it means four out of the six kleśas, since the mind is not associated with all six.**
- chosen: caturbhiḥ restricts the kleśas to four. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [389, 390]: `kleśā api ṣaṭ
na ca taiḥ saṃprayujyate 'taś caturbhir ity āha` — The afflictions too are six; and it is not associated with those [all], therefore he says ‘with four.’

**J4. The commentary glosses sahita directly with saṃprayukta, so it is stronger and more technical than loose accompaniment.**
- chosen: sahitam is translated as ‘conjoined/associated’ in the technical sense of saṃprayoga. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [391]: `sahitam iti saṃprayuktam` — ‘Sahitam’ means ‘associated/conjoined.’
- trbh [401]: `yāvat tad asti tāvat taiḥ saṃprayuktam` — As long as it exists, it is associated with them.

**J5. The four kleśas here are distinguished from akuśala kleśas; nivṛta is explained by being kliṣṭa, and avyākṛta by not being classed as wholesome or unwholesome.**
- chosen: nivṛtāvyākṛtaiḥ means ‘obscured-neutral’ or ‘afflicted yet ethically indeterminate’. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [392, 393, 394, 395]: `kleśā api dvividhāḥ
akuśalā nivṛtāvyākṛtāś ca
akuśalebhyo viśeṣārtham āha
nivṛtāvyākṛtair iti` — Afflictions too are of two kinds: unwholesome and obscured-neutral. To distinguish them from the unwholesome ones, he says ‘obscured-neutral.’
- trbh [396, 397, 398]: `na hi nivṛtena vijñānenākuśalānāṃ saṃprayogaḥ saṃbhavati
nivṛtāḥ kliṣṭatvāt
avyākṛtāḥ kuśalākuśalatvenāvyākaraṇāt` — For there is no association of unwholesome [factors] with an obscured cognition. They are obscured because they are afflicted; they are indeterminate because they are not determined as wholesome or unwholesome.

**J6. The scope is temporal and constant: whenever that mind exists, it is associated with these four.**
- chosen: sadā is translated as ‘always, so long as it exists’. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [399, 400, 401]: `sadeti
sarvakālam
yāvat tad asti tāvat taiḥ saṃprayuktam` — ‘Always’ means ‘at all times’: as long as it exists, it is associated with them.

**J7. The bhāṣya quotes ātmadṛṣṭi and glosses it by darśana and satkāyadṛṣṭi; the four names are self-view, delusion about self, self-conceit, and self-love/attachment.**
- chosen: Read ātmadṛṣṭi, not ātmaduṣṭi, and take the final compound as naming four specific kleśas. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [402, 403]: `sāmānyanirdeśād viśeṣato na jñāyanta iti viśeṣato nirdiśati
ātmadṛṣṭyātmamohātmamānātmasnehasaṃjñitaiḥ` — Since from a general designation they are not known specifically, he states them specifically: ‘called self-view, self-delusion, self-conceit, and self-love.’
- trbh [404, 405, 406, 407, 408, 409, 410]: `upādānaskandheṣv ātmeti darśanam ātmadṛṣṭiḥ satkāyadṛṣṭir ity arthaḥ
moho
'jñānam
ātmany ajñānam ātmamohaḥ
ātmaviṣaye māna ātmamāno 'smimāna ity
arthaḥ
ātmani sneha ātmasneha ātmapremety arthaḥ` — Seeing ‘self’ in the aggregates of clinging is self-view; the meaning is satkāyadṛṣṭi. Delusion is ignorance. Ignorance regarding self is self-delusion. Conceit with self as its object is self-conceit; the meaning is I-conceit. Affection/love toward self is self-love; the meaning is love for self.

**J8. Avoid a translation that implies the text affirms an ātman; the commentary frames these as confusion, self-view, conceit, and attachment concerning what is taken to be self.**
- chosen: The ‘self’ in the four names is not an accepted real self; in this context it is an object imagined as self, especially with respect to ālayavijñāna. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [411, 412, 413, 414, 415, 416]: `tatrālayavijñānasvarūpe
saṃmūḍhaḥ sann ālayavijñāne ātmadṛṣṭim utpādayati
ātmadarśanād yā
cittasyonnatiḥ so 'smimānaḥ
etasmiṃs traye saty ātmābhimate vastuni yo
'bhiṣvaṅgaḥ sa ātmasnehaḥ` — There, with regard to the own-nature of ālayavijñāna, being deluded, one produces self-view toward ālayavijñāna. The elevation of mind that arises from seeing self is I-conceit. When these three are present, the clinging to an object conceived as self is self-love.

## Translation

> Always, so long as it exists, this [mind (manas)]
is conjoined (saṃprayukta) with four obscured-neutral afflictions
(kleśa; nivṛtāvyākṛta):
those called self-view (ātmadṛṣṭi), delusion about self (ātmamoha),
self-conceit (ātmamāna), and self-love/attachment (ātmasneha).

## Analyzer disagreements

- ByT5 split nivṛtāvyākṛtaiḥ into nivṛta + avyākṛtaiḥ and tagged nivṛta as a separate participial item; I analyze the bhāṣya reading as one karmadhāraya compound stem nivṛtāvyākṛta, instr. pl. masc., because the commentary quotes nivṛtāvyākṛtair iti at trbh 395 and defines nivṛtāḥ/avyākṛtāḥ as two properties of the same kleśas at trbh 397–398.
- ByT5 supplied component Cp tags for the long final expression; I analyze ātmadṛṣṭyātmamohātmamānātmasnehasaṃjñitaiḥ as one inflected compound stem, instr. pl. masc., qualifying kleśaiḥ, because trbh 403 quotes it as the verse expression and trbh 404–410 unpack its four names.
- If ByT5’s sahitam_SAM is taken as accusative singular, I construe sahitam contextually as neuter nominative singular with the prior vijñānam/manas; the surface form is nominative/accusative singular homophonous.

## One-shot delta

- A commentary-blind translation might render nivṛtāvyākṛtaiḥ from the dictionary as ‘restrained/unmanifest and unexpounded’; Sthiramati instead makes it the technical class ‘obscured-neutral’, i.e. afflicted yet not classed as wholesome or unwholesome (trbh 392–398).
- It might take sahitam as loose ‘accompanied by’; the bhāṣya explicitly glosses it as saṃprayuktam, technical conjunction/association (trbh 391).
- It might miss that caturbhiḥ means four out of the six kleśas, not all afflictions or a generic fourfoldness (trbh 389–390).
- It might accept the vulgate ātmaduṣṭi or translate the long compound vaguely; the commentary reads ātmadṛṣṭi and defines all four names one by one (trbh 403–410).
- It might take ‘always’ as rhetorical; Sthiramati specifies ‘at all times’ as ‘as long as it exists, it is associated with them’ (trbh 399–401).
