## Justifications

**J1. Translate acitta not as “inconceivable” or merely “unconscious,” but as lacking grasper-mind; translate anupalamba as non-apprehension of the grāhya object.**
- chosen: acitta = absence of apprehender-mind; anupalamba = non-apprehension of the grasped object (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1507, 1508]: `grāhakacittābhāvād grāhyārthānupalambhāc
cācitto 'nupalambho 'sau` — Because of the absence of apprehender-mind and because of non-apprehension of the object-to-be-grasped, ‘that is acitta and anupalambha.’

**J2. Render lokottara as “world-transcending/supramundane,” not merely “extraordinary,” because Sthiramati derives it from having crossed beyond the world through non-worldly, non-conceptual occurrence.**
- chosen: lokottara = world-transcending supramundane knowledge (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1509, 1510]: `anucitatvāl loke samudācārābhāvān nirvikalpatvāc ca lokād uttīrṇam iti
jñānaṃ lokottarañ ca tad iti` — Because it is not customary, because it does not occur in the world, and because it is non-conceptual, it has crossed beyond the world; therefore, ‘that is supramundane knowledge.’

**J3. Take jñānam, lokottaram, and tat as nominatives: ‘that is supramundane knowledge,’ not as an accusative object.**
- chosen: jñānam lokottaram ca tat is a neuter nominative predication (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1510, 1511]: `jñānaṃ lokottarañ ca tad iti
tasya jñānasyānantaraṃ āśrayasya parāvṛttir bhavatīti jñāpanārtham āha` — ‘And that is supramundane knowledge’; to make known that after that knowledge the turning-about of the basis occurs, he says ...

**J4. Translate āśrayasya parāvṛttiḥ as the turning-about of the basis, with the basis specifically identified as the all-seed ālaya-vijñāna, and construe it as occurring after the lokottara jñāna.**
- chosen: āśraya = sarvabījaka ālayavijñāna; parāvṛtti follows that knowledge (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1511, 1513]: `tasya jñānasyānantaraṃ āśrayasya parāvṛttir bhavatīti jñāpanārtham āha
āśrayasya parāvṛttir iti
āśrayo 'tra sarvabījakam ālayavijñānam` — To indicate that after that knowledge the turning-about of the basis occurs, he says ‘the turning-about of the basis.’ Here ‘basis’ is the all-seed ālaya-vijñāna.

**J5. Render parāvṛtti as Yogācāra ‘turning-about/transformation,’ not merely physical turning or reversal.**
- chosen: parāvṛtti is a transformative turning involving cessation of the old dispositions and presence of fitness, dharmakāya, and nondual knowledge (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1514, 1516]: `tasya parāvṛttir yā
dauṣṭhulyavipākadvayavāsanābhāvena nivṛttau satyāṃ
karmaṇyatādharmakāyādvayajñānabhāvena parāvṛttiḥ` — Its turning-about is the turning-about in which, when cessation has occurred through the absence of the latent impressions of the two—dauṣṭhulya and vipāka—there is the presence of fitness, dharma-body, and nondual knowledge.

**J6. Construe dvidhā dauṣṭhulya-hānitaḥ as ‘through abandonment of the twofold dauṣṭhulya,’ not as a vague twofold abandonment or merely twofold parāvṛtti.**
- chosen: dvidhā qualifies dauṣṭhulya: the twofold dauṣṭhulya of the kleśa- and jñeya-obstructions (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1519, 1521]: `dvidhādauṣṭhulyahānitaḥ
dvidheti kleśāvaraṇadauṣṭhulyaṃ jñeyāvaraṇadauṣṭhulyañ ca
dauṣṭhulyam` — ‘Through abandonment of twofold dauṣṭhulya.’ ‘Twofold’ means the dauṣṭhulya of the afflictive obstruction and the dauṣṭhulya of the obstruction concerning what is to be known.
- trbh [1529]: `dvidhā āvaraṇabhedena sottarā niruttarā cāśrayaparāvṛttir uktā` — By the distinction of the obstructions, āśraya-parāvṛtti is said to be twofold: with a higher remainder and unsurpassed.

**J7. Translate dauṣṭhulya with the technical sense ‘unwieldiness/unfitness,’ not merely ‘grossness’ or ‘badness.’**
- chosen: dauṣṭhulya = the basis’s akarmaṇyatā and the seeds of the two obstructions (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1521, 1523]: `dauṣṭhulyam
āśrayasyākarmaṇyatā
tat punaḥ kleśajñeyāvaraṇayor bījam` — Dauṣṭhulya is the basis’s lack of fitness/workability; that, again, is the seed of the afflictive and knowable-obstructions.

**J8. Take hānitaḥ as ablatival-causal: the turning-about is obtained through the abandonment of the twofold dauṣṭhulya.**
- chosen: hānitaḥ = because of/from abandonment (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1517, 1519]: `sā punar āśrayaparāvṛttiḥ kasya prahāṇāt prāpyate
ata āha
dvidhādauṣṭhulyahānitaḥ` — Again, by the abandoning of what is that turning-about of the basis obtained? Therefore he says: ‘through abandonment of twofold dauṣṭhulya.’
- trbh [1525, 1528]: `āśrayaparāvṛttiḥ śrāvakādigatadauṣṭhulyahānitaś ca prāpyate yad āha
vimuktikāya iti
bodhisattvagatadauṣṭhulyahānitaś ca prāpyate yad āha
dharmākhyo 'yaṃ mahāmuner iti` — Āśraya-parāvṛtti is obtained through abandonment of dauṣṭhulya belonging to śrāvakas and so on—hence ‘liberation-body’; and it is obtained through abandonment of dauṣṭhulya belonging to bodhisattvas—hence ‘this is called dharma [body] of the Great Sage.’

## Translation

> It is without grasping mind (acitta), non-apprehension (anupalamba);
and that is world-transcending knowledge (lokottara-jñāna).
Then comes the turning-about of the basis (āśraya-parāvṛtti),
through the abandonment of the twofold unwieldiness (dauṣṭhulya).
