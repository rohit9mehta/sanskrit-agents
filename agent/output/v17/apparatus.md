## Justifications

**J1. vijñānapariṇāmo 'yam is not a generic ‘change of knowledge’; Sthiramati identifies it with the threefold transformation just taught.**
- chosen: ‘This transformation of consciousness’ is the immediately preceding threefold vijñānapariṇāma, and it is predicated as vikalpa. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1096]: `yo 'yaṃ vijñānapariṇāmas trividho 'nantaram abhihitaḥ so 'yaṃ vikalpaḥ` — ‘This threefold transformation of consciousness that was just stated is this vikalpa.’
- trbh [1100]: `tena trividhena vikalpenālayavijñānakliṣṭamanaḥpravṛttivijñānasvabhāvena` — ‘By that threefold vikalpa, whose nature is storehouse-consciousness, afflicted mind, and active consciousness …’

**J2. The commentary defines vikalpa through adhyāropa and identifies it with abhūtaparikalpa, the false construction constituted by three-realm cittas and caittas.**
- chosen: vikalpa is ‘conceptual construction/imagination’ bearing superimposed object-aspects, not ‘option’ or ‘alternative.’ (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1097, 1098, 1099]: `adhyāropitārthākārās traidhātukāś cittacaittā vikalpa ucyate
yathoktam
abhūtaparikalpas tu cittacaittās tridhātukā iti` — ‘The cittas and caittas belonging to the three realms, having the aspects of superimposed objects, are called vikalpa; as it has been said: “False construction consists of cittas and caittas of the three realms.”’

**J3. tena is construed instrumentally with vikalpyate, not as the causal ‘therefore’; the relative yad is filled out by Sthiramati as bhājana, ātman, skandhas, dhātus, āyatanas, rūpa, śabda, etc.**
- chosen: The first tena means ‘by it,’ i.e. by the threefold vikalpa; yad/tat refers to the imagined object (vastu). (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1100, 1101, 1102]: `tena trividhena vikalpenālayavijñānakliṣṭamanaḥpravṛttivijñānasvabhāvena
sasaṃprayogeṇa yad vikalpyate bhājanam ātmāskandhadhātvāyatanarūpaśabdādikaṃ
vastu tan nāstīty ataḥ sa vijñānapariṇāmo vikalpa ucyate` — ‘By that threefold vikalpa, whose nature is storehouse-consciousness, afflicted mind, and active consciousness, together with its associates, whatever object is imagined—receptacle-world, self, aggregates, elements, sense-bases, form, sound, and so on—that object does not exist; therefore that transformation of consciousness is called vikalpa.’
- trbh [1125, 1126]: `yasmāt pariṇāmātmakena vikalpena yad vikalpyate tena tan
nāsti tasmād viṣayābhāvāt sarvaṃ vijñaptimātrakam` — ‘Because what is imagined by the vikalpa whose nature is transformation—by it, that does not exist; therefore, because of the absence of an object-domain, everything is representation-only.’

**J4. Sthiramati argues for non-existent objects as supports: cognition can arise in illusion, dream, eye-disease, etc.; it arises from a prior same-kind consciousness, not from an external object. Thus the non-existence is targeted at the superimposed object.**
- chosen: tan nāsti denies the existence of the imagined object/ālambana/viṣaya, not the occurrence of consciousness itself. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1103]: `asadālambanatvāt` — ‘Because it has a non-existent object/support.’
- trbh [1108, 1109, 1110, 1111, 1112, 1113, 1114]: `māyāgandharvanagarasvapnatimirādāv asaty apyālambane jāyate
yadi ca
vijñānasyālambanapratibaddha utpādaḥ syād evaṃ sati māyādiṣv arthābhāvān na
vijñānam utpadyate
tasmāt pūrvakān niruddhāt tajjātīyavijñānād vijñānam utpadyate
na bāhyād
arthāt tasminn asaty api bhāvāt` — ‘Consciousness arises in illusion, a gandharva-city, dream, eye-disease, and the like, even when the object/support is unreal. If the arising of consciousness were bound to an object-support, then in illusions and the like, because there is no object, consciousness would not arise. Therefore consciousness arises from a preceding, ceased consciousness of the same kind, not from an external object, since it exists even when that is absent.’
- trbh [1119, 1120]: `tasmād adhyāropitarūpatvād vikalpasyālambanam asad iti
pratipattavyam` — ‘Therefore, because it has the nature of superimposition, the object/support of vikalpa is to be understood as non-existent.’

**J5. Sthiramati explicitly glosses tena as tasmāt and frames the sentence as avoiding the nihilistic extreme after rejecting superimposition.**
- chosen: The second tena, in tenedaṃ, means ‘therefore’ (= tasmāt), leading to vijñaptimātrakam because the object-domain is absent. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1121, 1122, 1123, 1124, 1125, 1126]: `anena tāvat samāropāntaṃ parihṛtyāpavādāntaparijihīrṣayā āha

**J6. The scope of ‘all’ and the force of mātrakam are fixed by explicit glosses: sarvam = traidhātukam plus asaṃskṛtam; mātra excludes what is over and above that, while ka fills the pāda.**
- chosen: sarvam includes the three realms and the unconditioned; mātra means ‘only’ by excluding any additional object-domain, and -ka is only metrical. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1127, 1128, 1129, 1130, 1131]: `sarvam iti traidhātukam

pādapūraṇārthaḥ` — ‘“All” means the three realms and the unconditioned. The word mātra serves to exclude an object-domain over and above that. The syllable ka is for filling out the pāda.’

## Translation

> This transformation of consciousness (vijñānapariṇāma) is conceptual construction (vikalpa):
whatever object is imagined by it—that is not there.
Therefore all this is representation-only (vijñaptimātraka),
‘all’ meaning the three realms and the unconditioned.

## Analyzer disagreements

- ByT5 lemmatizes vikalpyate as vikalpay; I analyze it as Karmani Lat 3 sg of vi-√kḷp, since the form is passive and the commentary construes yad vikalpyate as a passively imagined vastu (trbh 1101–1102).
- ByT5 prints the compound members vijñāna + pariṇāmaḥ and vijñāpti + mātrakam separately; per the required format I give the inflected compounds under the whole stems vijñānapariṇāma and vijñāptimātraka.
- ByT5 leaves the two occurrences of tena undifferentiated; the commentary distinguishes the first as instrumental ‘by the threefold vikalpa’ (trbh 1100–1101) and the second as causal ‘therefore’ = tasmāt (trbh 1124–1126).

## One-shot delta

- A commentary-blind rendering might take vikalpa in its common lexical sense ‘option/alternative’; Sthiramati instead defines it as false conceptual construction with superimposed object-aspects.
- It might translate both occurrences of tena the same way. The bhāṣya makes the first instrumental (‘by the threefold vikalpa’) and the second causal (‘therefore’ = tasmāt).
- It might read tan nāsti as simple nihilism. Sthiramati restricts the denial to the imagined object/support and then explicitly frames vijñaptimātra as avoiding the extreme of denial.
- It might leave sarvam vague or restrict it to conditioned three-realm experience; the commentary includes both the three realms and the unconditioned.
- It might treat -mātrakam as a substantive ‘measure’ or make -ka meaningful; Sthiramati says mātra excludes an additional object-domain and ka is only pādapūraṇa.
