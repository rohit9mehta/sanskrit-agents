## Translation

> Conceit (māna), the afflictive views (dṛg), and doubt (vicikitsā);
concealment (mrakṣa), spite (pradāśa), envy (īrṣyā)—then
stinginess (mātsarya), together with deceit (māyā).

## One-shot delta

- A commentary-blind translation might take mānadṛgvicikitsāḥ as a semantic compound such as ‘doubt about the view of conceit’; Sthiramati instead defines māna, dṛg, and vicikitsā separately and counts them among the six kleśas.
- It might render dṛg as ordinary ‘seeing’ or even as right view; the commentary restricts it to the five afflictive views and explicitly excludes laukikī samyagdṛṣṭi and anāsravā dṛṣṭi.
- It might translate māna by a dictionary sense such as ‘opinion’ or ‘measure’; Sthiramati’s gloss requires ‘conceit’, the elevation of mind that thinks itself superior.
- It might read īrṣyātha as a corrupted or variant īrṣyārtha; the bhāṣya quotation supports īrṣyā + atha, ‘envy; then …’.
- It might take mātsaryaṃ as an accusative or māyayā as ‘by illusion’; the commentary’s catalogue frame makes mātsaryaṃ a neuter nominative upakleśa and saha māyayā a comitative phrase.
