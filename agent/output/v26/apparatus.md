## Justifications

**J1. The first pāda sets the duration of non-establishment, and the third pāda gives the matching duration of non-cessation; it is not a quantitative “as much as ... so much.”**
- chosen: yāvat ... tāvat is a temporal correlative: “so long as ... so long.” (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1460, 1461, 1462]: `yāvad advayalakṣaṇe vijñaptimātratve yoginaś cittaṃ na
pratiṣṭhitaṃ bhavati tāvad grāhyagrāhakānuśayo na vinivartate na prahīyata ity
arthaḥ` — “So long as the yogin’s mind has not become established in representation-only, whose mark is non-duality, so long the underlying tendency of grasped-and-grasper does not cease, is not abandoned: this is the meaning.”

**J2. The locative is not “in mere information/request,” nor a vague “in consciousness-only” without the commentary’s ontological specification; Sthiramati glosses it with cittadharmatā and advayalakṣaṇa.**
- chosen: vijñaptimātratve means “in representation-only,” specifically the dharmatā of mind marked by non-duality. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1454, 1455]: `yāvac cittadharmatāyāṃ vijñaptimātrasaṃśabditāyāṃ vijñānaṃ
nāvatiṣṭhate` — “So long as consciousness does not abide in the dharmatā of mind that is designated ‘representation-only’ ...”
- trbh [1460]: `yāvad advayalakṣaṇe vijñaptimātratve yoginaś cittaṃ na` — “So long as the yogin’s mind is not [established] in representation-only, whose mark is non-duality ...”

**J3. The bhāṣya’s ātmanepada avatiṣṭhate is supported by the commentary’s own nāvatiṣṭhate and by the paraphrase pratiṣṭhitaṃ bhavati. The subject is not an accusative object but nominative neuter vijñānam, paraphrased as yoginaś cittam.**
- chosen: vijñānaṃ nāvatiṣṭhate is “consciousness/the yogin’s mind does not abide or become established.” (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1454, 1455]: `vijñānaṃ
nāvatiṣṭhate kiṃ tarhi grāhyagrāhakopalambhe carati` — “Consciousness does not abide. What then? It moves in apprehension of grasped and grasper.”
- trbh [1460, 1461]: `yoginaś cittaṃ na
pratiṣṭhitaṃ bhavati` — “The yogin’s mind has not become established.”

**J4. The compound is not ‘two graspers’ or the two entities themselves; Sthiramati defines it as two forms of grāha/upalambha, external and internal.**
- chosen: grāhadvaya is the twofold grasping: grasping of the grasped and grasping of the grasper. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1455, 1456, 1457]: `kiṃ tarhi grāhyagrāhakopalambhe carati
grāhadvayaṃ grāhyagrāho
grāhakagrāhaś ca` — “What then? It moves in apprehension of grasped and grasper. The twofold grasping is grasping of the grasped and grasping of the grasper.”
- trbh [1463, 1464]: `atra ca bahir upalambhāprahāṇenādhyātmikopalambhāprahāṇaṃ darśitam

**J5. The translation uses “latent seed,” not merely “attachment,” “remorse,” or an unspecified consequence, because the commentary explicitly defines anuśaya as bīja in ālayavijñāna.**
- chosen: anuśaya is a latent seed in the store-consciousness for the future arising of the twofold grasping. (depends on commentary: **yes**)

## Translation

> So long as the yogin’s consciousness (vijñāna) does not abide
in representation-only (vijñaptimātratva),
so long the latent seed (anuśaya) of the twofold grasping (grāhadvaya)—
grouting grasped object and grasper—does not cease.

- It could take grāhadvaya as “two graspers” or as object-and-subject themselves, rather than the two graspings/apprehensions: grāhyagrāha and grāhakagrāha.
