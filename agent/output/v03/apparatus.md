## Translation

> And that storehouse consciousness (ālayavijñāna)
has unapprehended appropriation (upādi)
and an unapprehended place-presentation (sthānavijñapti);
for as long as it is, it is accompanied
by contact (sparśa), attention (manaskāra), feeling (vedanā),
recognition (saṃjñā), and volition (cetanā).

## Analyzer disagreements

- ByT5 segmented the first compound as a-saṃvidita-kopa-ādi-sthāna-vijñaptikam. The commentary at trbh 203–205 parses it as asaṃviditaka upādiḥ ... asaṃviditakā sthānavijñaptiḥ, with upādi defined as upādāna; therefore kopa and ādi are not verse members here.
- ByT5 treated the privative a and saṃvidita as separate analyzed items. In the verse they are internal to the compound-member asaṃviditaka.
- ByT5 treated vid as an independent masculine nominative item meaning ‘knower/knowing’. The commentary states vedanā vit at trbh 242 and later asks which of the three vedanās vid denotes at trbh 302–303, so vid is the vedanā member of the compound.
- ByT5’s anvitam_anvita_SAM is overridden: anvitam is neuter nominative singular agreeing with tat/ālayavijñānam, as shown by trbh 203–204 and by the gloss yāvad ālayavijñānaṃ ... anvitam at trbh 239–241.

## One-shot delta

- A commentary-blind translation would likely split kopādi as kopa + ādi, yielding ‘anger etc.’; Sthiramati instead requires asaṃviditaka + upādi, with upādi = upādāna.
- It might translate vid as ‘knowing’ or ‘knower’; Sthiramati explicitly says vedanā vit, making it the mental factor feeling.
- It might take sthānavijñapti as a vague ‘place-cognition’; the bhāṣya identifies it as the presentation of the receptacle-world’s configuration and explains why it is unapprehended.
- It might render sadā simply as ‘always’ without scope; the commentary construes it as ‘for as long as ālayavijñāna exists, for that long’ it is accompanied by the five universal factors.
