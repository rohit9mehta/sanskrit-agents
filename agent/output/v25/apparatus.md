## Justifications

**J1. Do not translate paramārthaḥ merely as an ordinary “highest purpose/meaning.” Sthiramati gives a technical nirukti and then identifies it with pariniṣpanna-svabhāva, specifically as the paramārtha of all dharmas whose nature is dependent.**
- chosen: paramārthaḥ means the ultimate object/reality: the perfected nature as the object of supreme supramundane cognition and as the ultimate of dependent-nature dharmas. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1414]: `paramaṃ hi lokottarajñānaṃ niruttaratvāt tasyārthaḥ paramārthaḥ` — For supreme supramundane cognition, because it is unsurpassed, is ‘parama’; its object/referent is ‘paramārtha’.
- trbh [1415, 1416, 1417]: `atha
vākāśavat sarvatraikarasārthena vaimalyāvikārārthena ca pariniṣpannaḥ
svabhāvaḥ paramārtha ucyate` — Or, the perfected nature is called paramārtha in the sense that it is uniform everywhere like space, and in the sense of purity and non-alteration.
- trbh [1418, 1419, 1420]: `sa yasmāt pariniṣpannaḥ svabhāvaḥ sarvadharmāṇāṃ
paratantrātmakānāṃ paramārthaḥ
taddharmateti kṛtvā` — Because that perfected nature is the paramārtha of all dharmas, which have dependent nature, being their dharmatā.

**J2. The verse’s sa yatas is to be construed through the commentary’s sa yasmāt ... tasmāt, not as a spatial “whence.” The masculine pronoun is not referring to tathatā but to the perfected nature.**
- chosen: yatas is causal, “since/because,” and saḥ refers to pariniṣpannaḥ svabhāvaḥ. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1418, 1419, 1420, 1421]: `sa yasmāt pariniṣpannaḥ svabhāvaḥ sarvadharmāṇāṃ
paratantrātmakānāṃ paramārthaḥ
taddharmateti kṛtvā tasmāt pariniṣpanna eva
svabhāvaḥ paramārthaniḥsvabhāvātā pariniṣpannasyābhāvasvabhāvatvāt` — Because that perfected nature is the paramārtha of all dependent-nature dharmas, being their dharmatā, therefore the perfected nature itself is ultimate naturelessness, since the perfected has absence as its nature.

**J3. Translate tathatā as the technical term “suchness,” but do not make api concessive (“although”) or limit the point to one additional synonym. Sthiramati says api indicates that all dharmadhātu-synonyms may designate it.**
- chosen: tathatā api means “also suchness,” with api marking that this is not the only applicable designation. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1426, 1427, 1428, 1429]: `tathatāpi saḥ
apiśabdān na kevalaṃ tathatāśabdenaivābhidhātavyaḥ
kiṃ tarhi yāvanto
dharmadhātuparyāyāḥ sarvais tair apy abhidhātavya iti` — ‘It is also suchness.’ From the word api: it is not to be designated only by the word suchness; rather, by as many synonyms of dharmadhātu as there are, by all those too it is to be designated.

**J4. The compound is tathā-bhāva, “being so,” in the ablative of cause; it is not tathā-abhāva, “non-being so.” This line explains the name tathatā.**
- chosen: sarvakālaṃ tathābhāvāt means “because at all times it is just so.” (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1430, 1431, 1432, 1433]: `sarvakālaṃ tathābhāvāt
tathatā
tathā hi pṛthagjanaśaikṣāśaikṣāvasthāsu sarvakālaṃ tathaiva bhavati
nānyatheti tathatety ucyate` — ‘Because of being so at all times—suchness.’ For in the states of ordinary person, trainee, and adept, at all times it is just so, not otherwise; therefore it is called suchness.

**J5. Do not take vijñaptimātratā as a different entity or merely as the earlier ordinary thesis of cognition-only. Sthiramati explicitly poses the alternative—whether it is the perfected itself like tathatā, or something else—and answers with sā eva; he adds that this statement teaches realization (abhisamaya).**
- chosen: sā eva vijñaptimātratā identifies that very suchness/perfected nature with representation-only-ness. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1434, 1435, 1436, 1437]: `kiṃ punas tathatāvat pariniṣpanna eva vijñaptimātratā
utānyā vijñaptimātratā
ata āha
saiva vijñaptimātratā` — Is vijñaptimātratā, like tathatā, just the perfected, or is vijñaptimātratā something else? Therefore he says: ‘that very is vijñaptimātratā.’
- trbh [1444]: `saiva vijñaptimātratety anena vacanenābhisamaya uktaḥ` — By the statement ‘that very is vijñaptimātratā,’ realization has been taught.

## Translation

> Since that [perfected nature (pariniṣpanna-svabhāva)] is the ultimate reality (paramārtha) of dharmas (dharma),
it too is suchness (tathatā);
for at every time it is just so—
that very suchness is representation-only-ness (vijñaptimātratā).

## One-shot delta

- A commentary-blind translation could render paramārtha as a loose “highest purpose” and miss Sthiramati’s definition as the object of supreme supramundane cognition and as pariniṣpanna-svabhāva.
- It could misread tathatāpi as tathā api, “thus also/although,” whereas the bhāṣya treats it as tathatā api and explains apiśabda.
- It could miss the etymological force of tathābhāvāt—“because it is always just so, not otherwise”—or even split it wrongly as tathā-abhāvāt.
- It could take vijñaptimātratā as something distinct from suchness/perfected nature; Sthiramati explicitly rejects that by glossing sā eva.
