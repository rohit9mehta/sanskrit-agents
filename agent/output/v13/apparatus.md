**J1. Translate śāṭhya not as generic ‘wickedness’ but as a technical mental crookedness that functions by bewildering others so as to conceal one’s own fault.**

- trbh [841, 842, 843, 844, 845]: `śāṭhyaṃ svadoṣapracchādanopāyasaṃgṛhītañ cetasaḥ kauṭilyam
svadoṣapracchādanopāyaḥ paravyāmohanam

vikṣipaty aparisphuṭaṃ vā pratipadyate

- trbh [849, 850, 851, 852, 853]: `lābhasatkārādhyavasitopāyābhyāṃ rāgamohābhyāṃ svadoṣapracchādanārthaṃ

Beings are harassed by it through killing and binding and by the production of suffering and dejection. It assists all afflictions and secondary afflictions; since it is posited in a portion of delusion, it is delusion-related and not separately existent.

**J6. Although the supplied verse surface is uddhataḥ, Sthiramati comments on auddhatyaṃ; therefore the translation gives the technical mental factor ‘restlessness/non-quiescence’, not an ‘arrogant/uplifted man’.**

## Translation

> Deceitful crookedness (śāṭhya), self-intoxicated pride (mada), harmfulness (vihiṃsā);
shamelessness in oneself (ahrī/āhrīkya), no compunction before others (atrapā/anapatrāpya), torpor (styāna), restlessness (auddhatya);
then faithlessness (āśraddhya), sloth (kausīdya), heedlessness (pramāda),
and corrupted mindfulness—afflicted mindfulness (muṣitā smṛtiḥ).
