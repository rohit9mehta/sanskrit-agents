**J2. Although vikalpa can mean ‘alternative/option’ lexically, the verse pairs the noun vikalpaḥ with the passive verb vikalpyate; the context requires a process that constructs or imagines an object. ‘Option’ would not yield a coherent relative clause ‘what is optioned by it.’**
- chosen: vikalpaḥ / vikalpyate are rendered as ‘conceptual construction’ / ‘is conceptually constructed or imagined.’ (depends on commentary: no)
- trbh []: `vikalpo yad vikalpyate` — conceptual construction—what is constructed/imagined

**J3. Since vikalpyate is passive, yad is the grammatical subject of ‘is constructed,’ not an accusative object. The correlative tat is likewise nominative neuter singular and is the subject of nāsti. Thus the verse negates the existence of what is imagined, not the existence of the imagining transformation itself.**

## Translation

> This transformation of consciousness (vijñāna-pariṇāma) is conceptual construction (vikalpa):
what is constructed by it—that does not exist.
Therefore all this is representation-only (vijñapti-mātraka).
