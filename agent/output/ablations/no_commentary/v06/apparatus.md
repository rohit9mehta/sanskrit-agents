## Justifications

**J1. Grammatically, sahita is an adjective that can take an instrumental complement (“accompanied by/with,” MW s.v. sahita, 1195,1). It cannot agree with the plural kleśaiḥ, so the singular neuter form is best taken with the carried-over neuter subject: “it is accompanied by four afflictions.”**
- chosen: sahitam is predicative of an implicit neuter head; the instrumental plurals specify what it is accompanied by. (depends on commentary: no)

**J2. The compound is instrumental plural masculine, matching kleśaiḥ. As separate words, nivṛta would be morphologically out of agreement; as a compound, nivṛta + avyākṛta yields nivṛtāvyākṛta. Without commentary, its exact technical value remains provisional; the grammar supports “obstructed/restrained and undetermined/unexpounded.”**
- chosen: nivṛtāvyākṛtaiḥ is one adjectival compound qualifying the four kleśas. (depends on commentary: no)

**J3. The final member saṃjñita means “called, named, termed” when used in compounds (MW s.v. saṃjñita, 1133,3). Thus the preceding fourfold list supplies the names: “called self-view, self-delusion, self-conceit, and self-love.”**
- chosen: ātmadṛṣṭi, ātmamoha, ātmamāna, and ātmasneha are the four names of the afflictions. (depends on commentary: no)

**J4. The supplied bhāṣya reading has dṛṣṭi, and dṛṣṭi is lexically “seeing/view” (MW s.v. dṛṣṭi, 492,1). The vulgate ātmaduṣṭi is therefore not adopted for this apparatus.**
- chosen: ātmadṛṣṭi is read and translated as “self-view.” (depends on commentary: no)

**J5. The dictionary head is saṃjñita (MW s.v. saṃjñita, 1133,3), and the bhāṣya reading supplied for the verse has saṃjñitaiḥ. The variant sañjñitaiḥ is not adopted.**
- chosen: saṃjñitaiḥ is analyzed as the participial adjective from saṃjñita, “called/named.” (depends on commentary: no)

## Translation

> It is always accompanied by four afflictions (kleśa),
obstructed–undetermined (nivṛtāvyākṛta):
those called self-view (ātmadṛṣṭi), self-delusion (ātmamoha),
self-conceit (ātmamāna), and self-love (ātmasneha).
