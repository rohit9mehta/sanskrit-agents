## Justifications

**J1. The neuter nominative singular sarvabījam agrees with neuter nominative singular vijñānam. With MW s.v. sarva, “all, every,” and MW s.v. bīja, “seed” and “germ, primary cause or principle,” the compound is best taken as possessive/adjectival: consciousness is furnished with all seeds, not as an independent object “all seeds.”**
- chosen: sarvabījam = “all-seeded,” a bahuvrīhi adjective of vijñānam. (depends on commentary: no)
- trbh []: `sarvabījaṃ hi vijñānam` — for consciousness is all-seeded

**J2. The form pariṇāmaḥ is masculine nominative singular, and yāti is intransitive. This favors taking pariṇāmaḥ as the subject of the finite verb rather than as an accusative goal/object. The connection with vijñānam is contextual: the transformation is the transformation of the previously named consciousness.**
- chosen: pariṇāmaḥ is the nominative subject of yāti: “transformation proceeds.” (depends on commentary: no)
- trbh []: `pariṇāmas tathā tathā yāty` — transformation proceeds in such-and-such ways

**J3. MW s.v. tathā gives “in that manner, so, thus”; repetition naturally yields a distributive/variegated sense, coordinated with the later repeated demonstrative sa sa.**
- chosen: tathā tathā is a repeated adverbial: “in such-and-such ways.” (depends on commentary: no)
- trbh []: `tathā tathā` — in such-and-such ways

**J4. MW s.v. anyonya gives “one another, mutual,” and MW s.v. vaśa gives “authority, power, control.” The ablative singular -āt is therefore causal/source-like: the proceeding occurs because of, or under, mutual dependence/control.**
- chosen: anyonyavaśāt = “from mutual influence/dependence,” an ablative tatpuruṣa. (depends on commentary: no)
- trbh []: `anyonyavaśād` — from mutual influence/dependence

**J5. The instrumental relative pronoun links the preceding process of transformation with the arising of vikalpa. Since pariṇāmaḥ is masculine singular, masculine instrumental yena is a plausible agreement target, though the form is also neuter in isolation.**
- chosen: yena is instrumental: “whereby/by which.” (depends on commentary: no)
- trbh []: `yena vikalpaḥ sa sa jāyate` — whereby this or that conceptual construction arises

**J6. The repeated masculine nominative singular demonstrative agrees with vikalpaḥ and gives a distributive series of particular constructions rather than one single repeated item.**
- chosen: sa sa is distributive: “this or that / each corresponding.” (depends on commentary: no)
- trbh []: `vikalpaḥ sa sa jāyate` — this or that conceptual construction arises

**J7. Although jāyate is middle in form and can be rendered idiomatically “is born,” it functions here as an intransitive present of √jan with vikalpaḥ as nominative subject. The instrumental yena supplies cause/means, not a grammatical passive agent.**
- chosen: jāyate is kartari/intransitive: “arises,” not a passive “is produced” with an expressed agent. (depends on commentary: no)
- trbh []: `vikalpaḥ sa sa jāyate` — this or that conceptual construction arises

## Translation

> For consciousness (vijñāna) is all-seeded;
transformation (pariṇāma) proceeds in such-and-such ways,
through mutual influence;
whereby this or that conceptual construction (vikalpa) arises.
