## Justifications

**J1. vipāko mananākhyaś ca vijñaptir viṣayasya ca is not a list of unrelated items; it continues the previous verse’s trividhaḥ pariṇāmaḥ. The genitive viṣayasya is construed with vijñaptiḥ as the technical expression viṣaya-vijñapti.**
- chosen: The first half names the three kinds of vijñāna-pariṇāma: vipāka, mananākhya, and viṣayavijñapti. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [169]: `sa eṣa trividhaḥ pariṇāmo vipākākhyo mananākhyo viṣayavijñaptyākhyaś ca` — This very threefold transformation is called vipāka, called manana, and called viṣaya-vijñapti.

**J2. Translate vipāka as ‘maturation-result’ rather than merely ‘ripening’ or ‘cooking,’ because Sthiramati defines it through the ripening of wholesome and unwholesome karmic impressions and the production of their fruit.**
- chosen: vipāka = karmic maturation-result/fruition. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [170, 171]: `tatra kuśalākuśalakarmavāsanāparipākavaśād yathākṣepaṃ phalābhinirvṛttir
vipākaḥ` — Here, vipāka is the arising of a fruit according to projection, through the force of the ripening of impressions of wholesome and unwholesome karma.
- trbh [188]: `sarvadhātugatiyonijātiṣu kuśalākuśalakarmavipākatvād vipākaḥ` — It is vipāka because, in all realms, destinies, wombs, and classes of birth, it is the maturation-result of wholesome and unwholesome karma.

**J3. The compound is possessive, ‘having the name manana’; the term is not an adjective meaning simply ‘thoughtful’ and not a neutral ‘meditation.’**
- chosen: mananākhya = ‘called mentation,’ referring to the afflicted manas characterized by constant mentation. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [172]: `kliṣṭaṃ mano nityaṃ mananātmakatvān mananākhyam` — The afflicted mind is called manana because its nature is constant mentation.

**J4. The word vijñapti is not used here in the common sense ‘request/report’; Sthiramati defines it as the sixfold eye-and-so-on consciousness insofar as objects such as form appear.**
- chosen: vijñaptir viṣayasya = ‘presentation/manifestation of an object.’ (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [173, 174]: `rūpādiviṣayapratyavabhāsatvāc cakṣurādivijñānaṃ ṣaṭprakāram api
viṣayavijñaptiḥ` — Because objects such as form appear, the sixfold consciousness beginning with eye-consciousness is also viṣaya-vijñapti.

**J5. Translate tatra as ‘among these’ rather than simply ‘there.’ The ālaya-named consciousness is identified as the vipāka-pariṇāma.**
- chosen: tatra points back to the just-mentioned threefold pariṇāma; the second hemistich identifies the vipāka-pariṇāma. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [179, 180, 181]: `tatreti yo 'yam anantaroktas trividhaḥ pariṇāmaḥ
ālayākhyam ity
ālayavijñānasaṃjñakaṃ yad vijñānaṃ sa vipākapariṇāmaḥ` — ‘Tatra’ means this just-mentioned threefold transformation. ‘Ālayākhyam’ means: the consciousness that has the designation ālaya-vijñāna is the vipāka-transformation.

**J6. The English ‘storehouse/repository’ is chosen because Sthiramati glosses ālaya as sthāna, ‘place/support,’ and explains it as that in which all dharmas are bound as effects and which is bound to all dharmas as cause.**
- chosen: ālaya = repository/storehouse, not just a house or dwelling. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [183, 184]: `sarvasāṃkleśikadharmabījasthānatvād ālayaḥ
ālayaḥ sthānam iti paryāyau` — It is ālaya because it is the place of the seeds of all defiled dharmas. Ālaya and sthāna are synonyms.
- trbh [185, 186]: `atha vālīyante upanibadhyante 'smin sarvadharmāḥ kāryabhāvena tad vālīyate
upanibadhyate kāraṇabhāvena sarvadharmeṣv ity ālayaḥ` — Or, all dharmas are lodged/bound in it in the mode of effects, and it is lodged/bound in all dharmas in the mode of cause; therefore it is ālaya.

**J7. The translation ‘consciousness’ is supported by Sthiramati’s verbal gloss vijānāti iti vijñānam.**
- chosen: vijñāna = consciousness/discernment. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [187]: `vijānātīti vijñānam` — It is vijñāna because it cognizes/discerns.

**J8. Do not translate sarvabījakam as the noun ‘every seed’ or as merely ‘seed-like’; it qualifies ālayākhyam vijñānam as possessing/supporting all seeds.**
- chosen: sarvabījakam = all-seeded, i.e. the support of the seeds of all dharmas. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [189]: `sarvadharmabījāśrayatvāt sarvabījakam` — It is sarvabījaka because it is the support of the seeds of all dharmas.

## Translation

> The transformation is threefold:
maturation-result (vipāka), the one called mentation (manana),
and presentation of an object (viṣaya-vijñapti).
Among these, the consciousness called the storehouse (ālaya)
is the maturation-result, all-seeded (sarvabījaka).

## One-shot delta

- A commentary-blind translation could take vijñapti in its common MW sense ‘request/report,’ but Sthiramati defines it as object-presentation by the six sense-consciousnesses.
- It could translate tatra spatially as ‘there’; the commentary explicitly makes it an anaphoric ‘among these’ referring to the just-mentioned threefold transformation.
- It could miss that ālayākhyaṃ vijñānam is not just ‘a consciousness named abode’ but ālayavijñāna, the vipāka-pariṇāma, named ālaya because it is a repository/support for seeds.
- It could render sarvabījakam vaguely as ‘all-seed’ rather than ‘all-seeded,’ the support of the seeds of all dharmas.
