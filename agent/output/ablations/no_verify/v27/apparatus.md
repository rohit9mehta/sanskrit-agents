## Justifications

**J1. Read the verse as a corrective to ābhimāna based on mere hearing; this controls the concessive “even” in the translation.**
- chosen: The verse describes a conceited learner who thinks he is established in purified representation-only, not someone who has genuinely attained that state. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1472, 1473]: `iti atha vā yaḥ punar ābhimānikaḥ śrutamātrakeṇa jānīyad ahaṃ
vijñaptimātratāyāṃ śuddhāyāṃ sthita iti tadgrahavyudāsārtham āha` — Or else: someone conceited might, from mere hearing, think, “I am established in purified representation-only”; to remove that grasp, he says...
- trbh [1474, 1475]: `vijñaptimātram evedam ity api hy upalambhata ity
ādi` — ...the verse beginning “Even [thinking] ‘This is mere representation’—for he apprehends...”

**J2. Translate vijñaptimātram evedam as “This is mere representation alone,” not as a mere quantity of information or as a claim compatible with external objects.**
- chosen: vijñapti-mātra means objectless “mere representation,” excluding a bāhyārtha, an external object. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1476]: `vijñaptimātram evedaṃ artharahitaṃ na bāhyo 'rtho 'stīty evam` — “This is mere representation, objectless; there is no external object”—in this way...

**J3. Render with “as he apprehends—grasps and pictures,” while recognizing that this apprehension is precisely what has not yet been abandoned.**
- chosen: upalambhataḥ denotes problematic apprehension/grasping/mental picturing and supplies the reason he does not abide in representation-only. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1477]: `upalambhato grahaṇataḥ citrīkaraṇata ity artho` — upalambhataḥ means grahaṇataḥ, “from/by grasping,” and citrīkaraṇataḥ, “from/by making a mental image.”
- trbh [1481]: `vijñānopalambhāprahāṇāt` — Because the apprehension of consciousness has not been abandoned.

**J4. Take agrataḥ as “in front/facing,” sthāpayan as a mental act, and kiñcit as one among the many Yogācāra supports, not a real external thing placed physically before the practitioner.**
- chosen: agrataḥ sthāpayan kiñcit means mentally setting some practice-support before himself. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1477]: `ity artho 'grata ity abhimukhaṃ` — agrataḥ means abhimukham, “facing/in front.”
- trbh [1478]: `sthāpayann iti yathāśrutaṃ manasā` — sthāpayan means mentally, just as it has been heard/taught.
- trbh [1479, 1480]: `bahuprakāratvād yogācārālambanānāṃ kiṃcid ity āhāsthisaṃkalikaṃ vā vinīlakaṃ
vā vipūyakaṃ vā vipaḍumakaṃ vā vyādhmātakādikaṃ vā` — Because Yogācāra supports are of many kinds, by “something” he means, for example, a skeleton-support, a livid one, a festering one, a discolored one, a bloated one, and so on.

**J5. Connect tanmātra back to vijñaptimātratā rather than reading it as a Sāṃkhya subtle element or as “in that object alone.”**
- chosen: tanmātre is the locative “in that mere [representation],” i.e. in representation-only, and nāvatiṣṭhate is failure to be established there. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1473]: `vijñaptimātratāyāṃ śuddhāyāṃ sthita` — Established in purified representation-only.

**J6. Use the bhāṣya’s api hy upalambhataḥ rather than the vulgate-like hayu sequence.**
- chosen: The bhāṣya reading is api hi, yielding “for even,” with hi sandhied to hy before upalambhataḥ. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1474]: `vijñaptimātram evedam ity api hy upalambhata ity` — “Even [thinking] ‘This is mere representation’—for he apprehends...”

## Translation

> For even as he apprehends—grasps and pictures—
“This is mere representation (vijñapti-mātra) alone,”
mentally setting some support (ālambana) before him,
he does not abide in that mere [representation] (tanmātra).

## Analyzer disagreements

- ByT5 splits vijñaptimātram into vijñapti + mātram; I record the whole inflected compound stem vijñaptimātra, as required for compounds and as supported by the commentary’s vijñaptimātratā and gloss of vijñaptimātram.
- ByT5/local segmentation gives evā/eva around evedam; I analyze the printed form as regular eva + idam with a+i → e, not as an independent evā.
- ByT5 tags upalambhataḥ as a subanta of upalambha; a regular a-stem subanta would not yield -taḥ, and Sthiramati glosses it with ablatival -taḥ action expressions grahaṇataḥ and citrīkaraṇataḥ, so I analyze it as an indeclinable upalambhatas.
- ByT5 gives the verb lemma as avasthā; per instruction I record the bare root sthā with prefix ava for avatiṣṭhate.
- ByT5 gives sthāpayan with lemma sthāpay; I record the inflected participial nominal stem sthāpayat for the subanta analysis, while noting its causative origin.
- ByT5 segmentation has kiṃcid/kiñcid before tanmātre; the printed and pausal form here is kiñcit, and before t the final remains t.

## One-shot delta

- A commentary-blind translation might make this verse praise the cognition “all this is vijñapti-mātra”; Sthiramati says it targets an ābhimānika relying on mere hearing.
- It might treat the “something” placed in front as a real external object; the commentary makes it a mentally set practice-support among Yogācāra ālambanas.
- It might render upalambhataḥ as neutral or successful perception; Sthiramati glosses it as grahaṇa/citrīkaraṇa and says the problem is non-abandonment of vijñānopalambha.
- It might miss that vijñapti-mātra excludes external objects; the commentary explicitly glosses it as artharahita and says no bāhyārtha exists.
- It might take tanmātra as a Sāṃkhya subtle element or “that object alone”; the commentary ties it to abiding in purified vijñaptimātratā.
