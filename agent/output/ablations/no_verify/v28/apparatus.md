## Justifications

**J1. The commentary explicitly expands the pair with yasmin kāle and tasmin kāle, so I keep the temporal structure rather than making yadā causal or conditional in the main translation.**
- chosen: yadā ... tadā as a temporal correlative: “when ... then.” (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1486, 1488]: `yasmin kāle deśanālambanaṃ avavādālambanaṃ prākṛtaṃ vā rūpaśabdādyālambanaṃ
... tasmin kāle vijñānagrāhasya prahāṇaṃ` — “At the time when” there is such an object-support ... “at that time” there is abandonment of grasping cognition.

**J2. I translate “cognition does not apprehend an object-support,” not “one does not apprehend cognition” or “cognition is the object.” The commentary repeats exactly this construal and supplies examples of ālambana.**
- chosen: jñānam is the subject, and ālambanam is the apprehended object-support. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1486, 1487]: `yasmin kāle deśanālambanaṃ avavādālambanaṃ prākṛtaṃ vā rūpaśabdādyālambanaṃ
jñānaṃ bahiś cittān nopalabhate` — At the time when cognition does not apprehend, outside mind, a teaching-support, an instruction-support, or an ordinary support such as form, sound, and so on.

**J3. The translation uses “apprehend” and specifies “outside mind,” because the commentary glosses nopalabhate with seeing, grasping, and fixating, and places the denied object bahiś cittāt/cittān.**
- chosen: upalabhate means apprehends/perceives/graspingly notices, not obtains or acquires. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1487]: `jñānaṃ bahiś cittān nopalabhate na paśyati na gṛhṇāti nābhiniviśate` — Cognition does not apprehend outside mind, does not see, does not grasp, does not fixate.
- trbh [1488]: `yathābhūtārthadarśanān na tu jātyandhavat` — This is because of seeing reality as it is, not like someone blind from birth.

**J4. I take sthitam as a predicative nominative neuter referring to cognition/mind, not as an adjective modifying ālambanam. The locative is not a physical location but the Yogācāra state/own-nature of mind.**
- chosen: sthitaṃ vijñaptimātratve means “is established in representation-only,” glossed as being established in the own-nature of mind. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1488, 1489]: `tasmin kāle vijñānagrāhasya prahāṇaṃ
svacittadharmatāyāṃ ca pratiṣṭhito bhavati` — At that time there is abandonment of grasping cognition, and one/it is established in the own-nature of mind.
- trbh [1495, 1496]: `grāhyagrāhakābhiniveśānuśayāḥ prahīyante svacittadharmatāyāṃ ca cittam
avasthitaṃ bhavati` — The latent tendencies of fixation on grasped and grasper are abandoned, and mind becomes settled in the own-nature of mind.

**J5. The apparatus does not silently replace the bhāṣya’s jñānam/vijñaptimātratve with the vulgate vijñānam/vijñānamātratve. The commentary’s own prose continues with jñāna-language and glosses the state as svacittadharmatā.**
- chosen: The bhāṣya reading jñānam and vijñaptimātratve is followed, with vijñaptimātratva rendered “representation-only.” (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1484, 1485]: `yadā tv ālambanaṃ jñānaṃ naivopalabhate tadā | sthitaṃ vijñaptimātratve grāhyābhābhāve tadagrahāt` — When cognition does not apprehend an object-support, then it is established in representation-only, because of the non-grasping of that when the graspable-appearance is absent.
- trbh [1494]: `samasamālambyālambakaṃ nirvikalpaṃ lokottaraṃ jñānam utpadyate` — A nonconceptual supramundane cognition arises, with ālambya and ālambaka on the same footing.

**J6. Morphologically I retain the supplied bhāṣya form grāhya-ābhā-abhāva, but I do not translate it as mere optical darkness or “lack of splendour.” The commentary explains the reason through the absence of grāhya and consequent absence of grāhaka.**
- chosen: grāhyābhābhāve tadagrahāt is causal: when the graspable or its appearance is absent, there is no grasping of it and no grasper. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1490, 1491]: `atraiva kāraṇam āha
grāhyābhāve tadagrahād iti` — He states the reason for just this: because, when the graspable is absent, there is non-grasping of that.
- trbh [1492, 1493]: `grāhye sati grāhako bhavati na tu grāhyābhāva iti
grāhyābhāve grāhakābhāvam` — When the graspable exists, there is a grasper, but not when there is absence of the graspable; in the absence of the graspable, there is absence of the grasper.

## Translation

> When cognition (jñāna), however, does not at all apprehend
an object-support (ālambana) outside mind,
then it stands established in representation-only (vijñaptimātratva):
for, when the graspable-appearance (grāhyābhā) is absent,
there is no grasping of that.
