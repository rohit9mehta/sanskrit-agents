## Justifications

**J1. Reference of sarvabījaṃ vijñānam and the compound sense of sarvabīja.**
- chosen: “Consciousness” here means ālayavijñāna, qualified as “all-seeded” because it carries the powers/seeds for producing all dharmas. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1136]: `tatra sarvadharmotpādanaśaktyanugamāt sarvabījaṃ vijñānam ity ālayavijñānam` — Here, because it is accompanied by the power to produce all dharmas, ‘all-seeded consciousness’ means ālayavijñāna.
- trbh [1137, 1138, 1139]: `vijñānaṃ hy asarvabījam apy astīty ataḥ sarvabījam ity āha
vijñānād anyad
api kaiścit pradhānādi sarvabījaṃ kalpyata iti vijñānam ity āha` — Since there is also consciousness that is not all-seeded, he says ‘all-seeded’; and since some imagine something other than consciousness, such as pradhāna, to be all-seeded, he says ‘consciousness.’
- trbh [1140, 1141]: `atha vā
ekapadavyabhicāre 'pi viśeṣaṇaviśeṣyatvadarśanān nāyaṃ doṣaḥ` — Alternatively, even if a single word is non-exclusive, this is no fault, because a qualifier-qualified relation is observed.

**J2. Meaning of pariṇāma, force of yāti, and scope of tathā tathā.**
- chosen: pariṇāma is “alteration from a prior state,” and tathā tathā yāti means that this transformation reaches the state capable of immediately producing each respective vikalpa. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1142, 1143, 1144, 1145]: `pariṇāmas tathā tathā yāty anyonyavaśād iti
pūrvāvasthāto 'nyathābhāvaḥ pariṇāmaḥ
tathā tatheti tasya tasya
vikalpasyānantarotpādanasamarthāvasthāṃ prāpnotīty arthaḥ` — On ‘the transformation goes thus and thus because of mutual influence’: transformation is becoming otherwise than the previous state. ‘Thus and thus’ means ‘of each respective vikalpa’; the meaning is that it reaches a state capable of immediately producing that vikalpa.

**J3. Scope of “mutual influence/dependence” in anyonyavaśāt.**
- chosen: anyonyavaśāt is reciprocal conditioning: eye-etc. consciousness and the ālaya-consciousness transformation are each a condition (nimitta) for the other. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1146, 1147, 1148, 1149, 1150, 1151]: `anyonyavaśād iti
tathā hi cakṣurādivijñānaṃ svaśaktiparipoṣe vartamāne
śaktiviśiṣṭasyālayavijñānapariṇāmasya nimittaṃ so 'pi ālayavijñānapariṇāmaś
cakṣurādivijñānasya nimittaṃ bhavati
evam anyonyavaśād yasmād ubhayaṃ
pravartate tasmād ālayavijñānād anyenānadhiṣṭhitād anekaprakāro vikalpaḥ sa sa` — On ‘because of mutual influence’: when the nourishment of its own power is present, eye-etc. consciousness is a condition for the power-qualified transformation of ālayavijñāna; that ālayavijñāna-transformation too becomes a condition for eye-etc. consciousness. Thus, because both proceed through mutual influence, from that ālayavijñāna ...

**J4. Construal of yena, sa sa, vikalpaḥ, and the voice/sense of jāyate.**
- chosen: yena is rendered as correlative ‘whereby / by reason of which’; sa sa vikalpaḥ is distributive, ‘this or that construction,’ manifold and arising from ālayavijñāna without another governing basis. jāyate is therefore translated intransitively as ‘arises.’ (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [1150, 1151, 1152]: `evam anyonyavaśād yasmād ubhayaṃ
pravartate tasmād ālayavijñānād anyenānadhiṣṭhitād anekaprakāro vikalpaḥ sa sa
jāyate` — Thus, since both proceed through mutual influence, from that ālayavijñāna, not presided over by another, a manifold vikalpa, this or that, arises.
- trbh [1153, 1154]: `tatra ca vartamāne janmani yathālayavijñānāt
pravṛttivijñānasyotpattir bhavati tathākhyātam` — And there, with respect to the present life, how the arising of active consciousness occurs from ālayavijñāna has thus been explained.

## Translation

> For consciousness—store-consciousness (ālayavijñāna)—is all-seeded (sarvabīja):
by mutual influence its transformation (pariṇāma)
reaches such-and-such states,
and thereby this or that construction (vikalpa) arises.

## Analyzer disagreements

- ByT5 segments sarva_bījam; I analyze sarvabījam as one inflected compound stem sarvabīja, because the bhāṣya treats sarvabījaṃ vijñānam as a qualifier-qualified expression identifying ālayavijñāna (trbh 1136–1137, 1141).
- ByT5 segments anyonya_vaśāt; I analyze anyonyavaśāt as one inflected tatpuruṣa compound stem anyonyavaśa in the causal ablative, because the bhāṣya expounds anyonyavaśāt as reciprocal causal conditioning (nimitta) between cakṣurādivijñāna and ālayavijñānapariṇāma (trbh 1146–1150).

## One-shot delta

- A commentary-blind translation might take vijñāna as consciousness in general; Sthiramati explicitly identifies it as ālayavijñāna and explains why both sarvabīja and vijñāna are needed.
- It might render tathā tathā vaguely as “in various ways”; the bhāṣya specifies: the transformation reaches states capable of immediately producing each respective vikalpa.
- It might take anyonyavaśāt as vague mutual control or mutual influence among concepts; the commentary makes it the reciprocal nimitta-relation between cakṣurādi/prवṛtti-vijñāna and ālayavijñānapariṇāma.
- It might posit another governing basis or producer for vikalpa; Sthiramati says vikalpa arises from ālayavijñāna, anyenānadhiṣṭhitāt, “not presided over by another.”
