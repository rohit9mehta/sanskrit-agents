- trbh [577]: `eta ekādaśa dharmā iti vākyaśeṣaḥ` — The sentence is to be completed: these are eleven dharmas.
- trbh [679]: `uktā ekādaśa kuśalāḥ` — The eleven wholesome ones have been stated.

**J2. The compound is not left as a vague etcetera; the commentary’s sequence defines the three members immediately and distinctly.**

- trbh [601, 602, 603, 604, 605]: `alobho lobhapratipakṣaḥ

- trbh [607, 608, 609, 610, 611]: `adveṣo dveṣapratipakṣo maitrī

**J4. The translation avoids reducing praśrabdhi to simple calm or relaxation; Sthiramati defines it as the antidote to dauṣṭhulya and as kāya-citta-karmaṇyatā.**
- chosen: praśrabdhi is pliancy, the serviceability/workability of body and mind. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [625, 626, 627, 628, 629, 630, 631, 632, 633]: `praśrabdhir dauṣṭhulyapratipakṣaḥ kāyacittakarmaṇyatā

When that is removed, light arising occurs. The distinct mental factor that causes joy and lightness in a mind joined with correct attention, by virtue of which the mind proceeds unobstructed toward its object, is called workability of mind.

**J5. I do not construe sāpramādikā as an adjective of praśrabdhiḥ alone or as a stand-alone name for apramāda only. Sthiramati asks what the feminine referent is and answers upekṣā, then separately defines apramāda and upekṣā.**
- chosen: sāpramādikā points to upekṣā, equanimity, occurring together with apramāda, heedfulness. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [638, 639, 640, 641, 642, 643, 644, 645]: `sāpramādikā

Because it is exclusively wholesome, because all the wholesome factors are the topic here, because it is not directly mentioned like faith and the others, and because there is no other wholesome factor apart from these; therefore only equanimity is understood.
- trbh [646, 647, 648, 649, 650]: `tatrāpramādaḥ pramādapratipakṣaḥ

Apramāda is the factors from non-greed through energy: relying on those beginning with non-greed, one abandons unwholesome dharmas and cultivates the wholesome dharmas opposed to them; those beginning with non-greed are apramāda.
- trbh [654]: `upekṣā cittasamatā cittapraśaṭhatā cittānābhogatā` — Equanimity is evenness of mind, straightness of mind, and non-effort of mind.

**J7. The pāda boundary in subject matter comes after the eleven kuśalas. Sthiramati explicitly says the immediately following items are kleśas and introduces them with kleśā rāgapratighamūḍhayaḥ.**
- chosen: kleśāḥ begins a new category; rāga, pratigha, and mūḍhi are afflictions, not wholesome factors. (depends on commentary: **yes**)

## Translation

> The triad beginning with non-greed (alobha)—non-greed, non-hatred (adveṣa), non-delusion (amoha)—energy in the wholesome (vīrya), pliancy (praśrabdhi), equanimity (upekṣā) together with heedfulness (apramāda), and non-harming (ahiṃsā, glossed avihiṃsā): these complete the wholesome factors (kuśala). The afflictions (kleśa) are attachment (rāga), aversion (pratigha), and delusion (mūḍhi).

## Analyzer disagreements

- ByT5 left much of the first pāda as unknown or segmented, including alobha + ādi + traya and vīrya/praśrabdhi; the apparatus treats alobhāditrayaṃ as one neuter nominative singular compound, as required by the bhāṣya reading and by the list syntax.
- ByT5 segmented sāpramādikā as sā + apramādikā/tad + apramādika; the commentary explicitly has sāpramādikā and glosses it as sahāpramādena pravartata iti sāpramādikā, so it is analyzed as one feminine nominative singular from the corrected stem sāpramādika.
- ByT5 tagged āhiṃsā as a compound-form token. Here the pausal form is restored as ahiṃsā, the verifier-facing stem is corrected to ahiṃsa with feminine -ā, and the translation follows Sthiramati’s gloss avihiṃsā.
- ByT5 split rāga and pratigha as compound members and mūḍhayaḥ as the head. The apparatus records the whole verse-token rāgapratighamūḍhayaḥ as a dvandva compound stem rāgapratighamūḍhi.

## One-shot delta

- A commentary-blind translation could join kuśalāḥ kleśāḥ and produce the nonsensical phrase wholesome afflictions; Sthiramati marks the close of the eleven kuśalas and the beginning of the kleśas.
- It could take sāpramādikā as merely an adjective of praśrabdhiḥ or as apramāda alone, missing the supplied upekṣā and the separate explanation of apramāda.
- It could leave alobhāditrayaṃ as vague etcetera rather than the specific triad alobha, adveṣa, and amoha defined in order by the commentary.
- It could render vīrya as generic vigor or heroic strength, while Sthiramati restricts it to the mind’s ardor in wholesome dharmas.
- It could treat printed āhiṃsā as a separate long-vowel lemma; the apparatus restores pausal ahiṃsā and follows the commentary’s avihiṃsā, non-harming, gloss.
