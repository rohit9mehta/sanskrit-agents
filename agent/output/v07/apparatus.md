- trbh [441, 442]: `etair api yatra jātas tanmayair eva

- trbh [432, 433, 434, 435, 436, 437, 438]: `kiṃ punaś caturbhir eva kleśaiḥ saṃprayujyate

- trbh [443, 444, 445, 446]: `atha vānyair iti mūlavijñānasaṃprayuktebhyo vyavacchedārtham

**J4. arhataḥ is a genitive singular noun ‘of/for an arhat’, and tat refers to the kliṣṭaṃ manaḥ, not to an unspecified action or object of √arh.**
- chosen: arhato na tat = ‘for an arhat, that defiled mind is not’. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [447, 448, 449, 450, 451, 452, 453, 454]: `yadi tat kliṣṭaṃ manaḥ kuśalakliṣṭāvyākṛtāvasthāsv aviśeṣeṇa pravartate na

Would the absence of liberation not follow? It does not follow, because: ‘for an arhat that is not; nor in cessation-attainment, nor on the supramundane path.’ First, for an arhat, because all afflictions have been abandoned, the defiled mind simply does not exist.
- trbh [456, 457, 458]: `bhāvāgrikabhāvanāprahātavyakleśavad arhattvaprāptyānantaryamārgeṇaiva
prahīyate
tad anyakleśavad arhattvāvasthāyān naiva vidyate` — Like the afflictions of the summit of existence that are to be abandoned by cultivation, it is abandoned precisely by the uninterrupted path of attaining arhatship; like the other afflictions, it simply is not present in the state of arhatship.

**J5. The locative is the technical state nirodha-samāpatti, and the negation indicates non-operation/cessation there, not merely absence from a list.**
- chosen: na nirodhasamāpattau means that the defiled mind is stopped in the state of cessation-attainment. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [459, 460, 461, 462]: `ākiñcanyāyatanavītarāgasyāpy anāgāmino nirodhasamāpattilābhino mārgabalena

nirudhyate
nirodhāc ca vyutthitasya punar ālayavijñānād eva pravartate` — Even for a non-returner, dispassionate toward the sphere of nothingness and one who has obtained cessation-attainment, because cessation-attainment is obtained by the power of the path, it is stopped in the state of cessation-attainment as on the path; and when one has arisen from cessation, it operates again from the store-consciousness alone.

**J6. lokottare restricts mārge: Sthiramati explicitly says the mention of ‘supramundane’ excludes the worldly path, where the defiled mind does operate.**
- chosen: mārge lokottare na ca = ‘nor on the supramundane path’, specifically excluding the worldly path. (depends on commentary: **yes**)
- trbh [463, 464, 465, 466]: `mārge lokottare na ceti
lokottaragrahaṇaṃ laukikavyavacchedārtham
laukike tu mārge kliṣṭaṃ manaḥ
pravartata eva` — As for ‘and not on the supramundane path’: the word ‘supramundane’ is for excluding the worldly. But on the worldly path the defiled mind does indeed operate.
- trbh [467, 468, 469, 470]: `nairātmyadarśanasyātmadarśanapratipakṣatvān na lokottaramārge
pravartitum utsaheta
vipakṣapratipakṣayor ayaugapadyāl lokottaramārge tan
nirudhyate` — Because the vision of selflessness is the antidote to the view of self, it cannot manage to operate on the supramundane path. Since opponent and antidote cannot occur simultaneously, it is stopped on the supramundane path.

## Translation

> Wherever it is born, it is associated only with factors of that same realm (dhātu) and level (bhūmi);
for an arhat, that defiled mind (kliṣṭaṃ manas) is not.
Nor is it present in cessation-attainment (nirodha-samāpatti),
nor on the supramundane path (lokottara-mārga).
