# Sanskrit readers wanted: 30-verse translation evaluation (Yogācāra)

**Project.** Śāstrārtha is a research prototype that translates Sanskrit
śāstra by reading the text's own commentary before translating — every
contested choice is justified with a citation to the bhāṣya and every
morphological claim is checked against Pāṇinian grammar
(github.com/rohit9mehta/sanskrit-agents). We need human judges to compare
its translations against two machine-translation baselines.

**The task.** For each of the 30 verses of Vasubandhu's *Triṃśikā* (with
Sthiramati's bhāṣya as the underlying text), you read the Sanskrit, a
published human reference translation, and three anonymous machine
translations (labeled A/B/C, order randomized per verse). You answer three
questions per verse — which system best renders the technical terms as the
tradition understands them; which best resolves the compounds; which is
most faithful overall — in a provided CSV (ties allowed), with free-text
comments encouraged. No software setup: one Markdown file to read, one
spreadsheet to fill.

**You should have** reading proficiency in classical philosophical Sanskrit
(comfortable with bhāṣya-style prose and technical compounds). Familiarity
with Yogācāra terminology (vipāka, vijñapti, ālaya, kliṣṭa-manas, the three
svabhāvas) is strongly preferred — the eval turns on whether terms are
rendered in their technical senses. Graduate students in
Sanskrit/Buddhist studies, traditionally trained readers with English, and
computational-philology researchers are all welcome.

**Effort & compensation.** Estimated 3–6 hours, self-paced within ~2 weeks.
Honorarium offered (please propose your rate), plus acknowledgment by name
in any resulting publication (or anonymity if preferred).

**Integrity note.** The evaluation is blinded; the repository is public and
contains the label key — please don't consult the repo's eval directory
until you've submitted your responses.

**To participate**, reply with a sentence or two about your Sanskrit
background (e.g. texts you read regularly). We'll send the packet and
template directly.
